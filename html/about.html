Summary
My name is Raphael Reyna, and my background is in math/stats and higher education; however I find programming, in all of its forms, to be really fun. I'm also into electronics and 3D printing, and enjoy designing both the hardware and software of products. My preferred work environment is either macOS, Linux, or *BSD with a decent terminal, tmux, zsh, git and emacs; or a chalkboard.
Education
I currently hold a B.S. in Applied Mathematics and Statistics, along with a minor in physics; I am also currently working towards an M.S. in Pure Mathematics, which I expect to receive this summer. Outside of this, I have also attended and completed graduate courses in machine learning, data science, and time series. I have done research in fractal geometry and complex analysis, as well as given talks on the results.
Software
I became very interested in programming after taking a C++ course in college in 2011; I then was a TA for that same C++ course the next year. I was introduced to python in 2012, and used it throughout my math degree in order to takle mathematical problems quickly and efficiently. During my education I also gained experience in scientific computing, including using Mathematica and R/RStudio for research and data analysis, OpenCL and CUDA for high performance, parallelized computing on GPU's, and LaTeX for professionally communicating results. I enjoy all aspects of programming and have done so as hobby on my free time. Because of this I also have experience with app development on macOS and iOS, linux kernel module development on embedded devices, and web development.
Hardware
I was introduced to the Arduino platform in 2012 and have used it for several projects. Since then, I have also worked with embedded Linux on the Raspberry Pi and Rock64 SOCs; as well other platforms such as CircuitPython. I have experience in designing both hardware and software for IOT devices, for both consumer and industrial applications. In 2018, I obtained a 3D printer and have since gained experience in 3D printing and 3D CAD.
Work Experience
In my late teens I worked in a restaurant, taking on whatever role was needed at the time; while also working as a janitor in an office building on Sundays. Throughout my undergraduate degree I worked both as a tutor in various subjects; including math, physics, C++, Spanish, and Italian; and as a TA for math and C++ courses. Most recently, I have been working as a Math Instructor at the California State Polytechnic University, Pomona for the past 4 years.
